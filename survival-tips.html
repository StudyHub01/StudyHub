10 Minecraft Survival Tips Every Beginner Needs

From your first tree punch to your first diamond set -- the essential tips that separate beginners from survivors.

March 2026 All editions 10 tips

Minecraft's first night kills more new players than anything else. These 10 tips cover the most common beginner mistakes and how to avoid them, in order of priority from day one.

Most beginners stay on wood tools too long. Get to stone within the first 5 minutes, iron within the first day.

1
First 5 minutes

Punch trees and craft immediately

The moment you spawn, start punching the nearest tree to collect wood logs. You need at least 16 logs to start. Open your inventory, convert logs to planks, build a crafting table, and immediately craft a wooden pickaxe. Do not explore yet -- a Minecraft day lasts only about 10 real-world minutes, and you need tools and shelter before nightfall.

2
First 5 minutes

Upgrade to stone tools immediately

Wooden tools are only meant to get you to stone tools. Use your wooden pickaxe to mine 32 cobblestone blocks from any exposed rock face, then craft a full set of stone tools -- pickaxe, axe, shovel, and sword. Stone tools mine roughly twice as fast as wood and last much longer. Staying on wooden tools beyond the first few minutes slows everything down.

3
Before nightfall

Build a shelter before the sun sets

Your first shelter does not need to be impressive -- it just needs four walls, a roof, a door, and a torch inside. The easiest approach is to find a hillside and dig a small room into it. Place a door to keep mobs out and a crafting table and furnace inside. Most beginner deaths happen because players stayed outside after dark. Do not let that be you.

4
Day 1

Craft a bed and sleep through the night

You need 3 wool and 3 wood planks to craft a bed. Kill 3 sheep to get wool -- they are usually found on plains and forest biomes. Sleeping through the night skips the dangerous dark period entirely and sets your spawn point at your bed. If you die without a bed, you respawn at the world's original spawn point which could be very far from your base.

5
Day 1

Cook your food before eating it

Raw meat restores less hunger than cooked meat, and some raw food like rotten flesh causes hunger debuffs. Build a furnace from 8 cobblestone and use it to cook all your food. Cooked chicken, pork, beef and mutton restore significantly more hunger than raw. A furnace full of food is one of the best investments you can make early in the game.

6
Day 2

Get iron as fast as possible

Iron tools are dramatically better than stone in durability and speed. Iron armor is the difference between surviving your first cave and dying to the first zombie you meet. Mine at least 24 iron ore on day 2 -- enough for a full set of armor plus a pickaxe and sword. Iron is common between Y 15 and Y 232, so you do not need to go deep to find it.

7
Day 2

Place torches everywhere

Hostile mobs only spawn in darkness. Placing torches inside your base and along paths prevents creepers and zombies from spawning near you. When mining in caves, place torches every 8 to 10 blocks so you can find your way back and stop mobs from spawning behind you. Coal is common near the surface -- mine it for fuel and torches as early as possible.

8
Day 3

Find a village and start trading

Villages are one of the best early-game resources in Minecraft. They have farms with free food, chests with loot, and villagers you can trade with for emeralds and gear. Librarian villagers can sell enchanted books including Mending, which is one of the rarest and most valuable enchantments in the game. Mark your first village on a map or with a waypoint mod.

9
Always

Never dig straight down

This is the oldest rule in Minecraft and still one of the most broken by beginners. Digging straight down can drop you into a cave, a ravine, or directly into a lava pool. Always dig at a staircase angle so you can see what is below you before you fall into it. The same rule applies going up -- never dig directly upward as gravel or lava could fall on you.

10
Always

Start a farm as soon as possible

Hunting animals for food is not sustainable long term -- animals can run out in an area. A small wheat farm gives you a renewable food source that requires almost no maintenance. You need a hoe (craft from stone or iron), seeds from breaking grass, and tilled dirt near water. Plant wheat, wait a few in-game days, and you will have bread to eat without hunting. Expand with potatoes and carrots when you find them in villages.
