10 Minecraft Survival Tips Every Beginner Needs

From your first tree punch to your first diamond set -- the essential rules that separate survivors from beginners who keep dying.

March 2026All editions1.21 verified

Minecraft's first night kills more new players than anything else in the game. Not because it is unfair, but because most beginners do not know the right order of operations. These 10 tips cover everything you need, in order of priority, from the moment you spawn.

The tool progression rule -- never skip steps

Before the tips, understand the single most important rule in early Minecraft. Tool upgrades follow a strict order and skipping any step wastes significant time:

Tool progression order

Wood (first 5 minutes) -- Stone (next 5 minutes) -- Iron (day 1 goal) -- Diamond (week 1 goal) -- Netherite (endgame)

Most beginners stay on wood tools far too long. Get to stone within the first 5 minutes of spawning and iron by the end of day one. Everything accelerates from there.

Tip 1: Punch trees and craft tools immediately

The moment you spawn, start punching the nearest tree to collect wood logs. You need at least 16 logs to start. Open your inventory (E on Java, the inventory button on Bedrock), convert logs into planks, build a crafting table, and immediately craft a wooden pickaxe. A Minecraft day lasts about 10 real-world minutes. You need tools and shelter before it ends. Do not explore, do not fight mobs, do not get distracted.

Tip 2: Upgrade to stone tools within the first 5 minutes

Wooden tools exist only to get you to stone tools. Use your wooden pickaxe to mine 32 cobblestone blocks from any exposed rock face, then craft a full set of stone tools: pickaxe, axe, shovel, and sword. Stone tools mine roughly twice as fast as wood and last four times longer. If you are still using wooden tools after 5 minutes, you are playing too slowly.

Tip 3: Build a shelter before the sun sets

Your first shelter does not need to be impressive. It needs four walls, a roof, a door, and at least one torch inside. The easiest approach is to find a hillside and dig a small room into it. Place a door to block mob pathfinding and a crafting table and furnace inside. Creepers cannot open doors. Zombies can break them on Hard difficulty, but not on Normal or Easy. Most beginner deaths happen because players stayed outside after dark on their first night. Do not let that be you.

Tip 4: Craft a bed and sleep through the night

You need 3 wool and 3 wood planks to craft a bed. Kill 3 sheep to get wool -- they are usually found in plains and forest biomes. Sleeping through the night skips the dangerous dark period entirely and, more importantly, sets your spawn point at your bed. If you die without a bed in place, you respawn at the world's original spawn point which could be thousands of blocks from your base and your items.

Tip 5: Cook your food before eating it

Raw meat restores less hunger than cooked meat. Build a furnace from 8 cobblestone and cook all your food before eating it. Cooked chicken, pork, beef, and mutton restore significantly more hunger bars than their raw versions. The single exception is rotten flesh from zombies -- it causes a hunger debuff whether cooked or raw, so avoid eating it unless you are desperate.

Tip 6: Get iron armour as fast as possible

Iron armour is the difference between surviving your first cave and dying to the first zombie you encounter. Mine at least 24 iron ore on day 2 -- enough for a full armour set plus a pickaxe and sword. Iron is common between Y 15 and Y 232, so you do not need to go deep to find it. Any cave will have plenty of iron within the first few minutes of exploring. See our diamond guide for how to read coordinates to know your Y-level.

Tip 7: Place torches everywhere

Hostile mobs only spawn in darkness -- specifically, on blocks with a light level below 8. Placing torches inside your base and along all paths prevents creepers and zombies from spawning near you. When mining in caves, place torches every 8 to 10 blocks. Coal is extremely common near the surface. Mine it immediately whenever you see it -- you need fuel for your furnace and material for torches.

Tip 8: Find a village and start trading

Villages are one of the best early-game resources in Minecraft. They have farms with free food, chests with loot, and villagers you can trade with for emeralds and gear. Librarian villagers can sell enchanted books including Mending -- one of the rarest and most valuable enchantments in the game. When you find your first village, mark it on a map or set a waypoint using a mod like Xaero's Minimap. Use a seed from our seeds guide to spawn near a village from the very start.

Tip 9: Never dig straight down

This is the oldest rule in Minecraft and still one of the most broken by beginners. Digging straight down can drop you into a cave, a ravine, or directly into a lava pool with no warning. Always mine at a staircase angle so you can see what is below before you reach it. The same rule applies going up -- never dig directly upward as gravel or lava could fall on you instantly.

Tip 10: Build a farm as early as possible

Hunting animals for food is not sustainable long term -- animals can deplete in an area and respawn slowly. A small wheat farm gives you a renewable food source that runs itself. You need a hoe (craft from stone or iron), seeds from breaking grass, and tilled dirt placed within 4 blocks of a water source. Plant wheat, wait a few in-game days, harvest, and replant. Expand with potatoes and carrots from village farms when you find them. Once your food supply is secure, build an iron farm to automate your iron production too.

What to do after your first week

Once you have followed these 10 tips you should have iron armour, a working farm, a bed, and a safe base. The natural next steps are finding diamonds (see our diamond guide for exact Y-levels), setting up an enchanting table (see our enchantments guide), and automating your iron supply with an iron farm. After that, the Nether and eventually the End are your targets.
