Performance mods (install these first)

1. Sodium
Fabric Free 1.21+

The single most impactful mod you can install. Sodium completely overhauling Minecraft's rendering engine and dramatically increases frame rates -- players on low-end and mid-range PCs regularly report 2 to 5 times more FPS compared to vanilla. If your game stutters, drops below 60 FPS, or freezes when loading new chunks, install Sodium before anything else. It is free, lightweight, and compatible with almost every other Fabric mod available in 2026.

Download on Modrinth
2. Iris Shaders
Fabric Free 1.21+

Makes Minecraft look stunning with modern lighting, dynamic shadows, water reflections, and weather effects. Iris is the preferred shader loader for Fabric because it works alongside Sodium -- so you get both better performance and better visuals at the same time. Install Iris, then download a free shader pack. Complementary Shaders and BSL Shaders are both excellent free options that run well on mid-range hardware.

Quality of life mods

3. Just Enough Items (JEI)
Forge Fabric Free 1.21+

Shows you exactly how to craft every item in the game. Once you start using multiple mods, crafting recipes get complicated fast -- JEI solves this with a clean searchable interface that appears alongside your inventory. Hover over any item to see its recipe, or search for any ingredient to see everything it can be used to craft. Updated January 2026 and compatible with Minecraft 1.21. This is one of those mods you will never want to play without once you have used it.

Download on CurseForge
4. Xaero's Minimap
Forge Fabric Free 1.21+

Adds a minimap to the corner of your screen showing nearby terrain, players, mobs, and custom waypoints. In a game built around exploration, never getting lost is a genuine quality-of-life improvement. You can mark your base, your mine entrance, your villages, your iron farm, and fast-travel between them using the waypoint system. One of the most downloaded Minecraft mods in 2026 with millions of active users and regular updates. Works great alongside the survival tips in our beginner guide.

Download on CurseForge

5. AppleSkin
Forge Fabric Free 1.21+

Shows exactly how much hunger and saturation each food item restores. In vanilla Minecraft you can never tell which foods are actually worth eating -- a porkchop and a cookie both restore hunger bars but at completely different saturation values. AppleSkin fixes this with clear tooltips on every food item. It does not change any game mechanics, just gives you information the game should have shown from the start. Perfect for new players making food decisions.

Download on CurseForge

Exploration and inventory mods

6. Waystones
Forge Fabric Free 1.21+

Adds craftable fast-travel teleportation stones you can place around your world. Once you have a waystone at your base, your mine, your iron farm, and any villages you found, travelling becomes instant. In large survival worlds, walking long distances by foot gets old fast. Waystones solve it without breaking the game -- you still have to find or craft each waystone before you can use it. Placing a waystone in a village that generates with one also activates it automatically.

Download on CurseForge
7. Sophisticated Backpacks
Forge Fabric Free 1.21+

Adds craftable backpacks that massively expand your inventory while mining or exploring. You can upgrade them with modules including auto-smelting (smelts ore as you mine it), auto-sorting, and a built-in crafting table. Running out of inventory space during mining trips is one of the most frustrating parts of vanilla Minecraft -- this mod eliminates the problem entirely. One of the most downloaded inventory mods in 2026.

Download on CurseForge

Content mod

8. Biomes O' Plenty
Forge Fabric Free 1.21+

Adds over 80 new biomes to Minecraft, making exploration feel genuinely exciting again. Volcano islands, lavender fields, mystic groves, crystal chasms, redwood forests -- each biome has unique plants, trees, and terrain generation. If you have played vanilla Minecraft for a long time and feel like you have seen everything, this mod makes the world feel brand new. One of the oldest and most loved content mods in the community, continuously updated since 2012.

Download on CurseForge

How to install Minecraft mods

Beginner recommendation

For beginners, use the Modrinth App or CurseForge launcher. Both handle downloads, version compatibility, and updates automatically so you never have to touch any files manually.

  • Method 1 -- Mod launcher (recommended for beginners)

    Download the Modrinth App or CurseForge launcher and install it. Create a new Minecraft profile inside the app, select your Minecraft version and mod loader (Fabric or Forge), search for mods by name, and click Install. Launch Minecraft through the app and all mods load automatically. No manual file management required.

  • Method 2 -- Manual install

    Install the Fabric or Forge mod loader first from their respective websites. Download your chosen mod files in .jar format from CurseForge or Modrinth. Press Windows key + R, type %appdata%, open the .minecraft folder, and drop the mod files into the mods folder. Launch Minecraft using the Fabric or Forge profile in your Minecraft launcher.
