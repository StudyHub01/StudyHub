How to Build an Iron Farm in Minecraft 1.21

A simple beginner-friendly iron farm that produces 330+ iron ingots per hour on Java. No redstone needed. Works on Java and Bedrock.

March 2026Java and Bedrock1.21 verified330+ iron per hour

Iron is the most useful resource in early and mid-game Minecraft. You need it for tools, armour, hoppers, rails, anvils, and dozens of crafting recipes. An iron farm eliminates the grind entirely -- once built, it produces iron automatically while you do other things.

How iron farms work

Iron golems spawn naturally in villages when villagers feel threatened. When a zombie gets close enough to frighten your villagers, they enter a panicked state and trigger iron golem spawns more frequently. An iron farm traps villagers and a zombie together so the villagers are permanently scared. The golems spawn on a platform above them, fall into a lava kill chamber below, and drop iron ingots into a hopper connected to a chest.

The key requirements are at least 3 villagers (Java) or 10 villagers (Bedrock) with beds linked to them, a zombie to trigger the panic state, a spawning platform, and a kill chamber to collect drops. Everything else in this guide is the practical steps to build these components.

Java vs Bedrock

Java Edition needs only 3 villagers. Bedrock Edition needs at least 10 villagers and a larger spawning platform. The steps are otherwise similar. See the comparison table below for full details.

Important before you start

Build this farm at least 80 blocks away from any existing village. Too close and the villagers may link to the nearby village instead of your farm's beds, and the farm will not work.

Step by step build instructions

  • Find a flat area 80+ blocks from any village

    Choose a flat spot or clear one. Proximity to an existing village is the most common reason iron farms fail, so check your map before you start. If you used a seed from our seeds guide, note the village location and build away from it.

  • Dig the villager chamber

    Dig a 3x3x3 room underground. This is where your villagers will sleep. Place your beds inside -- all 3 (or 10 for Bedrock) need to fit fully inside the room with both the foot and head of each bed placed. Leave a viewing gap at the top so golems can detect the villagers and the villagers can see the zombie in the next chamber.

  • Build the zombie chamber

    Next to the villager chamber, dig a small room for the zombie. Place a boat in this room -- boats trap mobs inside them when they walk into them, which is the easiest way to contain a zombie without it escaping or burning in sunlight. The zombie chamber needs line of sight to the villager chamber. A 1 to 2 block gap between the two chambers works well.

  • Build the spawning platform

    Above the villager chamber, build a flat platform. Iron golems spawn in a 16x6x16 area around the village centre, so extend the platform at least 8 blocks in every direction from the centre of the beds. Keep the surface completely flat with no obstructions -- golems need a solid block underneath and 2 blocks of empty space above to spawn.

  • Build the kill chamber and collection system

    Below the spawning platform, build a channel that funnels golems to a single drop point. Use water streams to push them toward a 1-block wide hole. At the bottom, place a chest, connect a hopper to the chest pointing upward, and place a single lava block above the hopper area. The lava kills golems as they fall and the hopper automatically collects the dropped iron ingots. Golems drop 3 to 5 iron ingots and 0 to 2 poppies when killed.

  • Add the villagers first (before the zombie)

    Transport 3 villagers from a nearby village using a boat or minecart. Do not add the zombie yet. With the zombie present, villagers panic and refuse to enter the farm or link to the beds. Once all villagers are inside and have linked to the beds -- you will see green particles when they do, usually at night -- move on to the next step.

  • Add the zombie last

    Wait for nightfall, find a zombie, and lead it into the zombie chamber while wearing armour so it attacks you and follows. Once it walks near the boat it gets trapped inside. Seal the chamber. Make sure the entire zombie area has a solid roof -- underground placement is safe, but any exposure to sky will kill the zombie when the sun rises.

  • Test and verify

    Once the zombie is in place, villagers should start panicking. Within 1 to 2 minutes you should hear iron golem spawning sounds and see golems appearing on the platform. Stay within 128 blocks of the farm for it to remain active -- if you walk too far away, the chunks unload and the farm stops. Check the chest after a few minutes to confirm iron ingots are collecting.

Troubleshooting

Golems are not spawning

Check that your villagers have linked to the beds -- wait for nightfall and watch for green particles above them. Make sure the spawning platform has no solid blocks in the 2-block space above it. Check you are within 128 blocks of the farm. If villagers have not linked, try breaking and replacing the beds one at a time.

Villagers will not enter the farm

Make sure the zombie is not already in place. The zombie's presence panics villagers and makes them refuse to move normally. Always add villagers first, confirm they link to beds, then add the zombie afterwards.

The zombie keeps dying

Your zombie chamber is exposed to sunlight. Make sure the entire zombie area has a solid roof. Alternatively, give the zombie any helmet -- a zombie wearing a helmet will not burn in sunlight. Drop the helmet near it and the zombie will pick it up automatically.

Iron ingots are not collecting in the chest

Check that the hopper is connected correctly. Crouching (Shift on Java) and right-clicking a chest while placing a hopper connects them. The hopper input should face upward to catch drops falling from above.

The farm stopped working after a Minecraft update

Updates occasionally adjust villager pathfinding and bed linking. Breaking and replacing beds usually fixes unlinking. Also verify the zombie is still alive in its chamber and has not been killed by a bug or entity crowding.
