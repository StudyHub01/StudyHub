How to Build an Iron Farm in Minecraft 1.21

A simple beginner-friendly iron farm that produces 330+ iron ingots per hour. No redstone needed. Works on Java and Bedrock.

March 2026 Java and Bedrock 1.21 verified 330+ iron per hour

Iron is the most useful resource in Minecraft. You need it for tools, armor, hoppers, rails, and dozens of other crafting recipes. Instead of spending hours mining, an iron farm gives you a renewable supply automatically. This guide walks you through a simple beginner-friendly design that works in Minecraft 1.21 and produces over 330 iron ingots per hour on Java Edition.

How iron farms work

Before building, it helps to understand the basic mechanic. Iron golems spawn naturally in villages when villagers feel threatened. When a zombie gets close enough to scare your villagers, they panic and trigger iron golem spawns more frequently. The farm traps villagers and a zombie together so the villagers are constantly scared, maximising the spawn rate. The golems then fall into a lava kill chamber and drop iron ingots into a hopper and chest below.

The key requirements are: at least 3 villagers with beds linked to them, a zombie to scare them, a spawning platform for the golems, and a kill chamber to collect the drops. That is the entire mechanic -- everything else in this guide is just the practical steps to set it up.

Java vs Bedrock: On Java Edition you only need 3 villagers. On Bedrock Edition you need at least 10 villagers and a larger spawning platform. The rest of the steps are similar. A dedicated Bedrock section is at the bottom of this guide.

Step by step build guide

1

Find a flat area and dig the pit

Find a flat area at least 80 blocks from any village. Dig a 3-block wide channel that is 7 blocks deep. This channel will house the villager chamber, the zombie chamber, and the kill area underground. Keeping everything underground makes the farm compact and keeps the villagers safe from other mobs wandering in from outside.

2

Build the villager chamber

At the bottom of your pit, dig out a 3x3x3 room. This is where your 3 villagers will sleep. Place 3 beds inside this room -- the villagers need to be able to pathfind to and sleep in the beds for the farm to work. Leave a gap at the top so golems can see the villagers and the villagers can see the zombie in the next chamber. The beds need to be fully placed (both foot and head of the bed) inside the chamber.

3

Build the zombie chamber

Next to the villager chamber, dig out a small room for the zombie. Place a boat in this room -- boats trap mobs inside them when they walk into them, which is the easiest way to contain a zombie without it escaping or burning in sunlight. The zombie chamber needs to have line of sight to the villager chamber so the villagers can see the zombie and enter a panicked state. A gap of 1 to 2 blocks between the chambers works well.

4

Build the spawning platform

Above the villager chamber, build a flat spawning platform. Iron golems spawn in a 16x6x16 area around the village center, so your platform should extend at least 8 blocks in every direction from the center of the beds. Keep the platform flat with no obstructions -- golems need a solid block underneath and 2 blocks of empty space above them to spawn. The platform should be level with or just above the top of the villager bunker.

5

Build the kill chamber and collection system

Build a channel or funnel below the spawning platform that leads golems into a small kill area. Use water streams to push the golems towards a 1-block wide drop point. At the bottom, place a hopper connected to a chest, then place a single lava block above the hopper -- the lava kills the golems and the hopper collects the dropped iron ingots automatically. The golems drop 3 to 5 iron ingots and 0 to 2 poppies when killed.

6

Add the villagers

Lead 3 villagers from a nearby village to your farm using a boat or by luring them. Do not add the zombie yet -- if the zombie is already there, the villagers will panic and refuse to enter the farm or link to the beds. Once all 3 villagers are inside and have linked to the beds (you will see green particles when they do), you can move on to the next step. Villagers usually link to beds at night, so wait until nightfall if they have not linked yet.

7

Add the zombie

Now lure a zombie from the surface into the zombie chamber. The easiest method is to wait until night, find a zombie, and lead it down into the farm while wearing armour. Once it walks near the boat it will get trapped inside. Seal the zombie chamber so it cannot escape. The zombie must be protected from sunlight -- if the chamber is underground it is safe. If any part is exposed to the sky, place a roof over the zombie area.

8

Test the farm

Once the zombie is in place, the villagers should begin panicking (you will hear villager distress sounds). Within a minute or two, iron golems should start spawning on the platform above. They will be pushed by the water streams into the kill area, killed by the lava, and iron ingots will collect in the chest. Stay within 128 blocks of the farm for it to remain active -- if you walk too far away the chunks unload and the farm stops producing.

Pro tip: Without a zombie, the farm still works but produces significantly less iron -- around 50 to 100 ingots per hour instead of 330+. The zombie is what makes the big difference in output rate.

Troubleshooting

Golems are not spawning

Check that your villagers have linked to the beds -- wait for nightfall and watch for green particles. Make sure the spawning platform has no solid blocks in the 2-block space above it. Also check you are within 128 blocks of the farm. If villagers have not linked to beds, try breaking and replacing the beds.

Villagers are not entering the farm

Make sure the zombie is not already in place when you are trying to get villagers in. The zombie's presence causes villagers to panic and refuse to move normally. Add villagers first, confirm they link to beds, then add the zombie.

The zombie keeps dying

Your zombie chamber is likely exposed to sunlight. Make sure the entire zombie area has a solid roof above it. Alternatively, give the zombie a helmet -- a zombie wearing any helmet will not burn in sunlight. You can drop a helmet near it and it will pick it up automatically.

Iron ingots are not collecting in the chest

Check that the hopper is connected to the chest correctly -- the hopper should point into the chest, not just sit next to it. To connect a hopper to a chest, crouch and right-click the chest while placing the hopper. The hopper input should face upward to catch drops falling from above.

Farm is too slow or stopped working after an update

Check that villagers still have valid beds and have not unlinked. Minecraft updates occasionally adjust villager pathfinding which can cause them to unlink. Breaking and replacing beds usually fixes this. Also confirm your zombie is still alive and in its chamber.

Golems are spawning outside the kill area

Your spawning platform may have blocks the golems can escape onto. Make sure the platform edges funnel golems toward the water stream. You can add fences or walls along the edges to prevent escapes. Also check that the water streams flow in the right direction toward the drop point.
