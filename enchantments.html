Enchanting Guide 2026

Best Minecraft Enchantments for Beginners

Mending, Fortune, Protection and more. What they do, how to get them, and what order to prioritize first.

March 2026Java and Bedrock1.21 verified

Enchantments transform your gear from basic survival tools into permanent investments. Knowing which ones to get first saves hours of grinding and makes every subsequent activity -- mining, farming, combat -- dramatically more efficient.

How to set up your enchanting table

  • Craft the enchanting table

    You need 4 obsidian, 2 diamonds, and 1 book. Obsidian is created when water touches lava source blocks. Two diamonds is a manageable cost -- see our diamond guide for how to find them quickly.

  • Surround it with exactly 15 bookshelves

    On its own the enchanting table only gives level 1 to 8 enchantments. Surrounding it with 15 bookshelves placed one block away (with one air block between each shelf and the table) unlocks the maximum level of 30. Each bookshelf costs 3 books and 6 wooden planks. Books require leather from cows or horses.

  • Stock up on lapis lazuli

    Enchanting costs lapis lazuli. It spawns most commonly around Y 0 in any biome. Mine a full stack before setting up your enchanting room -- you will go through it faster than you expect.

  • Build an XP farm eventually

    Enchanting and repairing gear at an anvil requires experience levels. A mob XP farm or a manual grinder near a spawner accelerates everything. For now, mining and fighting mobs provides enough XP to get started.

Must-have enchantments

Mending
Applies to: all tools, weapons, and armour pieces
Must have

The single most valuable enchantment in Minecraft. Mending repairs your gear using experience orbs you collect, which means a well-maintained set of gear lasts indefinitely. Without it, every tool eventually breaks and you craft a new one, losing all other enchantments on it. With it, a good set of gear is permanent. Mending cannot be obtained from the enchanting table -- it is a treasure enchantment. You must get it from a librarian villager trade, a loot chest, or fishing. Getting Mending should be your first major goal after setting up the enchanting table.

Unbreaking III
Applies to: all tools, weapons, and armour pieces
Must have

Makes your gear last roughly 4 times longer by giving each use a 75% chance to not consume durability. Essential on pickaxes and armour. Unbreaking III effectively quadruples the life of every tool and piece of armour you put it on. It is obtainable from the enchanting table at level 30 and is relatively common. Put it on every piece of gear you intend to keep long term. Combined with Mending, your gear becomes permanent.

Fortune III
Applies to: pickaxe
Must have

Multiplies ore drops. With Fortune III a single diamond ore block can drop up to 4 diamonds instead of 1. It also works on coal, lapis lazuli, emeralds, redstone, and nether quartz. Over a full mining session this multiplies your yield by 2 to 4 times. Fortune III is incompatible with Silk Touch -- you can only have one on a pickaxe at a time. Keep a separate Silk Touch pickaxe for collecting ore blocks to break later with Fortune III.

Protection IV
Applies to: all armour pieces
Must have

Reduces all incoming damage by a flat percentage. Protection IV on all four armour pieces makes you dramatically harder to kill and protects against fire, explosions, projectiles, and falls -- far more versatile than Fire Protection or Blast Protection which cover only specific damage types. Put it on your chestplate and leggings first as those absorb the most damage. Getting all four armour pieces to Protection IV is a major power spike.

High priority enchantments

Efficiency V
Applies to: pickaxe, axe, shovel, hoe
High priority

Makes mining dramatically faster. Efficiency V on a diamond or netherite pickaxe allows you to mine most blocks almost instantly. Combined with Unbreaking III and Mending it creates the perfect permanent mining pickaxe. This single enchantment saves hours over a full survival playthrough. Efficiency only applies when using the correct tool for the block type -- pickaxe on stone, shovel on dirt, and so on.

Sharpness V
Applies to: sword and axe
High priority

Increases melee damage significantly. Sharpness V works against all mob types making it the most versatile weapon enchantment. If you primarily fight undead mobs -- skeletons, zombies, and the Wither -- Smite V deals even more damage specifically to them. But for general survival, Sharpness V is more broadly useful and recommended as your default weapon enchantment.

Feather Falling IV
Applies to: boots
High priority

Reduces fall damage by 48% at level IV. Fall damage is one of the most common ways experienced players lose their gear on a bad day. Feather Falling IV on boots makes you far more survivable when exploring caves, mountains, and the End. According to the Minecraft Wiki, trying to get Feather Falling from the enchanting table at levels 22 to 26 gives a higher chance than trying at level 30.

Good to have enchantments

Silk Touch
Applies to: pickaxe
Good to have

Makes blocks drop themselves instead of their normal resources. Silk Touch lets you collect diamond ore blocks to break later with Fortune III for maximum diamonds. It also lets you collect grass blocks, ice, bookshelves, and beehives intact. Keep a dedicated Silk Touch pickaxe alongside your Fortune III pickaxe -- these enchantments are incompatible and cannot exist on the same tool.

Thorns III
Applies to: armour
Good to have

Deals reflected damage to attackers when they hit you. Thorns III on your chestplate passively damages mobs that attack you, which is useful against large groups. The downside is that Thorns increases durability loss on your armour, so it is less useful without Mending and Unbreaking already in place. Get those first, then add Thorns.

How to get Mending (the hardest and most important)

Important

Mending cannot be obtained from the enchanting table. It is a treasure enchantment and only comes from specific sources.

  • Librarian villager trading (most reliable)

    Find an unemployed villager, place a lectern in front of them to make them a librarian, and check their trades. If Mending is not available, break and replace the lectern -- villager trades reset when their job site block changes. Repeat until Mending appears. This works because each librarian randomly selects enchanted books to offer. This method requires no luck beyond finding a village. Use our seeds guide to spawn near a village.

  • Fishing with Luck of the Sea III

    A fishing rod enchanted with Luck of the Sea III increases your chance of catching treasure enchantment books including Mending. AFK fishing setups can produce Mending books consistently. Slower than the villager method but works in worlds without nearby villages.

  • Loot chests in structures

    Ancient cities, end cities, and bastion remnants all have a chance of containing Mending books in their chests. End cities are particularly reliable -- nearly every end city ship has an item frame with an enchanted item and chests with books.
