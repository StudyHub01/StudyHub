Enchanting Guide 2026

Best Minecraft Enchantments for Beginners

What to get, what to skip, and how to set up your enchanting table for maximum level enchantments.

March 2026 Java and Bedrock 1.21 verified

Enchantments transform your gear from basic to unstoppable. Knowing which ones to prioritize first saves hours of grinding. This guide covers the essential enchantments for beginners -- what they do, how to get them, and what order to aim for them.

How to set up your enchanting table

Step 1 -- Craft an enchanting table

  1. You need 4 obsidian, 2 diamonds, and 1 book
  2. Place the enchanting table down in your base
  3. On its own it only gives you level 1 enchantments -- you need bookshelves

Step 2 -- Surround it with 15 bookshelves for level 30 enchantments

  1. Place 15 bookshelves around the table with exactly one block of air between them and the table
  2. This unlocks the maximum enchantment level of 30
  3. Each bookshelf costs 3 books and 6 wooden planks to craft
  4. Books require leather -- kill cows or horses to get leather

Important: You need lapis lazuli to enchant. It is a blue ore found at Y levels 0 to 32, most commonly around Y 0. Mine a full stack before setting up your enchanting room.

Must-have enchantments

Mending

Applies to: all tools, weapons, and armor
Must have

The single most valuable enchantment in the game. Mending repairs your gear automatically using experience orbs you collect. Without it, every tool eventually breaks and you have to craft a new one. With it, a good set of gear lasts indefinitely as long as you keep gaining XP. Mending cannot be obtained from the enchanting table -- you must get it from a librarian villager trade, a loot chest, or fishing. It is worth hunting for before anything else.

Unbreaking III

Applies to: all tools, weapons, and armor
Must have

Makes your gear last roughly 4 times longer by giving each use a 75% chance to not consume durability. Essential on pickaxes and armor. Unbreaking III effectively quadruples the durability of tools and weapons. It can be obtained from the enchanting table and is relatively common at level 30. Put this on every piece of gear you plan to keep long term.

Fortune III

Applies to: pickaxe
Must have

Multiplies drops from ore blocks. With Fortune III, a single diamond ore can drop up to 4 diamonds instead of 1. It also works on coal, lapis lazuli, emeralds, redstone, and nether quartz. Fortune III is incompatible with Silk Touch -- you can only have one on a pickaxe. If you already have Silk Touch on a pickaxe, keep a second pickaxe with Fortune III for mining valuable ores.

Protection IV

Applies to: all armor pieces
Must have

Reduces all incoming damage by a flat percentage. Protection IV on all four armor pieces makes you dramatically harder to kill. It protects against fire, explosions, projectiles, and falling objects -- much more versatile than Fire Protection or Blast Protection which only cover specific damage types. Put Protection IV on your chestplate and leggings first as they take the most hits.

Efficiency V

Applies to: pickaxe, axe, shovel, hoe
High priority

Makes mining dramatically faster. Efficiency V on a diamond or netherite pickaxe allows you to mine most blocks almost instantly. This saves hours of time over the course of a survival playthrough. Combined with Unbreaking III and Mending it becomes the perfect long-term mining tool. Efficiency only works when using the correct tool for the block -- a pickaxe on stone, a shovel on dirt, and so on.

Sharpness V

Applies to: sword and axe
High priority

Increases melee damage significantly. Sharpness V adds 3 extra damage per hit (1.5 hearts) on Java Edition. It works against all mobs making it the most versatile weapon enchantment. If you are fighting a lot of undead mobs like skeletons, zombies, and the Wither, Smite V deals even more damage against them specifically -- but Sharpness is more useful in general survival situations.

Feather Falling IV

Applies to: boots
High priority

Reduces fall damage by 48% at level IV. Fall damage is one of the most common ways experienced players lose gear. Feather Falling IV on boots makes you much more survivable when exploring caves, mountains, and the End dimension. One tip from the Minecraft Wiki: when trying to get Feather Falling from the enchanting table, use level 22 to 26 rather than 30 -- the chance of getting it is much higher at those levels.

Silk Touch

Applies to: pickaxe
Good to have

Makes blocks drop themselves instead of their resources. Silk Touch lets you collect diamond ore blocks to break later with Fortune III for maximum diamonds. It also lets you collect grass blocks, ice, bookshelves, and beehives intact. Keep a Silk Touch pickaxe alongside your Fortune III pickaxe -- they are incompatible so you cannot have both on the same tool.

How to get Mending (the hardest one)

Important: Mending cannot be obtained from the enchanting table. It is a treasure enchantment, which means it only comes from specific sources.

Best ways to get Mending

  1. Librarian villager trading -- the most reliable method. Find an unemployed villager, place a lectern in front of them to make them a librarian, then check their trades. If Mending is not available, break and replace the lectern until it appears. This works because villager trades reset when their job site block changes.
  2. Fishing -- using a rod with Luck of the Sea III increases your chance of catching enchanted books including Mending. Slower but works without a village nearby.
  3. Loot chests -- ancient cities, end cities, and bastion remnants have a chance of containing Mending books.
